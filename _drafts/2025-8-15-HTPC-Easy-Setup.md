Are you looking for a lazy girl setup on your tiny questionable mini pc you bought for $100 on amazon? Look no further!

Here is what I wanted my mini pc to do:
1. have some sort of launcher to launch my favorite things: Youtube, Netflix and other streaming sites
2. Run linux so that I can control things from my phone and send files/links/images via KDE 
3. Play music and games. 

I tried many linux distros like Fedora, Bazzite and Ubuntu but ultimately landed on kubuntu. I did not like Bazzite because it just felt like fedora with steam in it. I tried fedora vanilla but I was missing the connectivity to kde. 

Unfortunately, at the time of setting things up, plasma bigscreen was deprecated. It seems to be up now while I edit this from the drafts so will configure and report back on that if I feel like it (this is the lazy girl guide after all). 

In lieu of having bigscreen, I configured firefox to have a new tab page with large icons using the [Yet Another Speed Dial](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/yet-another-speed-dial/) extension for firefox. 

All other (relevant) firefox extensions:
- [Ublock Origin](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/ublock-origin/)
- [Adaptive Tab Bar Color](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/adaptive-tab-bar-colour/)
- [SponsorBlock](https://sponsor.ajay.app/)

I also use [LocalSend](https://localsend.org/) to share things from my iPhone/Mac to my HTPC because KDE is pretty glitchy these days. 

[Stremio](https://www.stremio.com/) is excellent for streaming torrents and aggregating from many torrent sites. Here is a link to a site compiling addons for it: https://stremio-addons.net/.[^1]

Most of our games are on Steam but we also play TuxCart for fun sometimes. 

We have Spotify installed and connected to large speakers for playing music throughout the room. 

We use a keyboard with a trackpad to control everything as eventually phone KDE got annoying. 

[^1]: I do not condone piracy and this is purely for educational purposes.
